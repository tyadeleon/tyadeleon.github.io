100 Years of Hair Removal

1920s:

First woman razor in 1915. Shaving underarm hair became popular due to the access of women razors and the sleeveless flapper style trend rising in the 1920s.

1930s:

During the 1930s women removed hair painfully through the use of pumice stones or sandpaper.

1940s:

Women began to use the newest form of hair removal, an electric razor.

1950s:

Thin, high arched brows were all the rage. Many people took inspiration from trendsetters Marylin Monroe and Clara Bow. Women tweezed their eyebrows to match.

1960s:

By the 60s, waxing as a form of hair removal became popular. Women removed body hair from their legs and underarms.

1970s:

Nair hair removal cream became popular.

1980s:

Waxing by this time was still popular but the Brazilian wax was popularized.

1990s:

Laser hair removal was introduced during this time ensuring smooth skin for some time or even indefinitely after a few treatments.

2000s:

Laser hair removal was now available for at-home use!
